Off-Ball Flex Screen and Wing Reset

Advanced Coaching Report
Coach's Analysis

This play features a high-priority down screen from the SF to facilitate a baseline-to-wing cut for the PG. Timing is critical, as the PF and C provide spacing to ensure the defense cannot collapse into the paint during the screen transition.
Step-by-Step Breakdown

    1SF sets a firm down screen in the mid-post area to create separation for the primary cutter.
    2PG utilizes the SF screen to curl towards the wing for a perimeter reset or drive opportunity.
    3The ball is swung from the high post through the PG to the SG in the opposite corner to stretch the defense.
    4C and PF maintain high and low post positioning to occupy help-side defenders.
